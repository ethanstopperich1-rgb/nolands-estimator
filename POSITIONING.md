## The one-sentence positioning

**Voxaris turns any roofing website into a 24/7 appointment machine,
white-labeled as the contractor's brand.**

That's it. That's the job. Everything else — the estimator, Sydney
voice AI, SMS follow-up, AEO website, JobNimbus push — is in service
of that one outcome: **booked appointments, 24/7, on their brand.**

---

## What we are NOT

- ❌ A lead-gen company
- ❌ An EagleView competitor
- ❌ "Unlimited roof reports"
- ❌ A "one-stop shop"

All of those frames anchor on **features** instead of the **outcome**
the roofer is actually buying. We sell the outcome.

---

## What the product actually does (end-to-end)

A complete top-of-funnel revenue machine. The estimator is the entry
point of an automated pipeline that takes a stranger on a website to
a booked appointment without a human touching it:

1. Homeowner lands on the contractor's white-labeled subdomain
2. Types their address → gets an EagleView-quality roof report in
   under 30 seconds
3. Sydney (AI voice receptionist) calls within ~10 seconds if they
   opt in
4. SMS follow-up sequence on every lead, opted-in or not
5. Rep notified instantly via SMS + dashboard alert
6. Appointment books into JobNimbus (or whatever CRM the office uses)
7. The roofer wakes up to **booked jobs, not missed calls**

## Who's buying, and what's in their head

**Buyer:** roofing contractor or franchise owner.

**Job To Be Done:** booked appointments with pre-measured homeowners.
Not estimates. Not voice AI. Not websites. The appointment is the
only thing they pay for everything else to produce.

**Their current stack** (the one we replace):
- EagleView for reports ($30-$100 each, universally resented)
- A website agency for the site
- A receptionist or answering service for after-hours
- Manual follow-up or a basic CRM

Each piece feels "fine." Switching feels like risk. **The positioning
has to make the current stack feel like the thing costing them money
— which it is.**

## The sales sequence (foot-in-the-door, not bundle pitch)

**Three SKUs:**

- **Estimator** (the wedge): $1,500 setup + $1,795/mo
- **Voice AI receptionist** (Sydney): $1,200 setup + $1,495/mo
- **Website + AEO Framework v3.2**: $1,495 setup + $1,495/mo

**Full bundle:** ~$4,800/mo + ~$4,200 setup → ~$57k ARR per
contractor.

**DO NOT pitch the bundle in cold outreach.** The foot-in-the-door
psychology works against you if you do.

The right order:

1. **Lead with Estimator only** — "try your address, 30 seconds."
2. **Once they're a customer, upsell Sydney**: "leads are flowing —
   want Sydney to call them within 10 seconds so you stop losing the
   after-hours ones?"
3. **Then upsell AEO website**: "your competitors are getting
   answered in ChatGPT/Perplexity searches. Here's how to get there
   too."

Each yes makes the next yes easier. Commitment + consistency.
Collapsing this into one upfront bundle pitch flips the psychology
against you.

## Why white-label is the moat

White-labeling is not a feature — it's a **psychological lock-in
mechanism**.

Once a contractor's brand is on the subdomain, their ads point to
it, their yard signs carry the QR code → switching costs become
enormous. The IKEA effect (they built it) plus the endowment effect
(it's theirs now) means churn approaches zero.

**They've built their lead machine on top of us.** That's the moat,
and it's psychological as much as it is technical.

## The 3-phase business arc (don't skip phases)

- **Phase 1 (now → 12 mo)**: vertical SaaS to contractors. Get 5-20
  paying FL offices. Each deployment is data ingestion.
- **Phase 2 (12-18 mo)**: aggregate-insights surface on
  `pitch.voxaris.io`. Cross-deployment data powers homeowner-facing
  comparisons.
- **Phase 3 (24-36 mo)**: transparent marketplace. Homeowner
  accounts, contractor bidding, real close-rate data. The
  transparent alternative to Angi/HomeAdvisor, only possible because
  phases 1+2 built the ground-truth foundation Angi can't reproduce.

**Don't run B2C homeowner marketing until phase 2.** The aggregate
dataset needs depth first (signal: cross-office price norms are
stable + statistically meaningful at the zip-code level).

## What changes when the positioning changes — checklist

When updating any customer-facing surface, apply this filter:

- [ ] Does it lead with the **outcome** (booked appointments), not a
      feature (estimator / voice AI / website)?
- [ ] Is the loss-aversion frame ("missed calls becoming competitor
      jobs") present somewhere visible?
- [ ] Is white-label called out — homeowner sees the contractor's
      brand, not ours?
- [ ] Is the ask specific ("try your address at pitch.voxaris.io")?
- [ ] Are we anchoring against EagleView + agency + answering service
      cost, not just against "free"?
- [ ] Is the bundle hidden from cold outreach? Cold lead-in is
      Estimator only.

If any answer is no, rewrite.
